Saudi Arabia, officially known as the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia (KSA), is the largest country in the Middle East and the birthplace of Islam. Located on the Arabian Peninsula, Saudi Arabia plays a central role in the Islamic world, with its cultural, historical, and religious significance shaping both regional and global affairs.

Iran, officially known as the Islamic Republic of Iran, is a country located in the heart of the Middle East, bordered by Armenia, Azerbaijan, Turkmenistan, Afghanistan, Pakistan, Turkey, and Iraq. It also has coastlines along the Caspian Sea to the north and the Persian Gulf to the south. With a rich history that spans millennia, Iran is one of the world's oldest continuous civilizations, and its cultural, political, and geopolitical influence continues to resonate globally.

The United Kingdom (UK), officially known as the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland, is a sovereign country located in northwestern Europe. The UK consists of four countries: England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland. It has played a significant role in world history, both as a global power during the era of the British Empire and as a leading force in shaping the modern world, particularly in the areas of politics, economics, culture, and international relations.
